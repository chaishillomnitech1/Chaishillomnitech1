## 3. Risk-Based Approach

### 3.1 Risk Assessment Methodology

Chais Protocolâ„¢ adopts a risk-based approach to AML/CFT compliance:

**Customer Risk Factors:**
- Type of customer (individual, corporate, trust)
- Nature of business or occupation
- Country of residence or incorporation
- Transaction patterns and volumes
- Source of wealth and funds

**Product/Service Risk Factors:**
- NFT value and type
- Transaction size and frequency
- Delivery channels (on-chain, off-chain)
- Payment methods

**Geographic Risk Factors:**
- High-risk jurisdictions identified by FATF
- Countries subject to sanctions
- Jurisdictions with weak AML/CFT controls

### 3.2 Risk Ratings

**Low Risk:**
- Standard CDD procedures
- Regular monitoring
- Annual review

**Medium Risk:**
- Enhanced CDD procedures
- More frequent monitoring
- Semi-annual review

**High Risk:**
- Enhanced due diligence (EDD)
- Continuous monitoring
- Senior management approval required
- Quarterly review

## 4. Customer Due Diligence (CDD)

### 4.1 Identification Requirements

**For Individuals:**
- Full legal name
- Date of birth
- Nationality
- Residential address
- Government-issued photo ID (passport, national ID, driver's license)
- Proof of address (utility bill, bank statement - not older than 3 months)

**For Corporate Entities:**
- Legal name and registration number
- Registered address and principal place of business
- Certificate of incorporation
- Memorandum and articles of association
- Beneficial ownership information (individuals owning â‰¥25%)
- Authorization documents for account signatories

**For Trusts and Foundations:**
- Trust deed or foundation charter
- Trustee information
- Settlor and beneficiary information
- Beneficial ownership structure

### 4.2 Verification Methods

**Tier 1 (Basic) - Up to SGD 5,000:**
- Email verification
- Wallet address verification
- Basic identity information

**Tier 2 (Standard) - Up to SGD 20,000:**
- Government-issued ID verification (automated + manual review)
- Proof of address verification
- Sanctions screening
- PEP screening

**Tier 3 (Enhanced) - Unlimited:**
- Video verification (liveness check)
- Enhanced sanctions and PEP screening
- Source of funds verification
- Enhanced monitoring

### 4.3 Ongoing Due Diligence

**Regular Reviews:**
- Low Risk: Annually
- Medium Risk: Semi-annually
- High Risk: Quarterly

**Trigger Events for Re-verification:**
- Significant change in transaction patterns
- Change in customer circumstances
- Regulatory list updates (sanctions, PEPs)
- Passage of verification expiry period

## 5. Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD)

### 5.1 EDD Triggers

EDD is required for:
- Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs) and their associates
- Customers from high-risk jurisdictions
- Transactions exceeding SGD 20,000
- Complex or unusual transactions
- Customers flagged by internal risk assessment

### 5.2 EDD Measures

Additional measures include:
- Senior management approval for business relationship
- Enhanced verification of source of wealth and funds
- More frequent and intensive monitoring
- Additional due diligence on beneficial owners
- Purpose and intended nature of business relationship
- Ongoing enhanced monitoring

### 5.3 Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs)

**Definition:**
- Individuals entrusted with prominent public functions
- Immediate family members of PEPs
- Close associates of PEPs

**PEP Categories:**
- Foreign PEPs
- Domestic PEPs
- International organization PEPs

**Additional Requirements:**
- Senior management approval required
- Establish source of wealth and funds
- Enhanced ongoing monitoring
- Consider additional measures based on risk assessment

## 6. Transaction Monitoring

### 6.1 Automated Monitoring

**Real-Time Screening:**
- Sanctions list screening (OFAC, UN, EU, Singapore)
- PEP screening
- Adverse media screening
- Blockchain address analysis

**Transaction Pattern Analysis:**
- Unusual transaction amounts
- High-frequency transactions
- Structuring patterns
- Cross-border flows
- Transactions inconsistent with customer profile

### 6.2 Alert Management

**Alert Generation:**
- Automated rule-based alerts
- Machine learning anomaly detection
- Threshold-based alerts
- Behavioral analytics

**Alert Review Process:**
1. Initial automated triage
2. Analyst investigation
3. Risk assessment
4. Decision (clear, escalate, file STR)
5. Documentation

**Response Timeframes:**
- Critical alerts: Within 1 hour
- High priority: Within 24 hours
- Medium priority: Within 3 business days
- Low priority: Within 5 business days

### 6.3 Monitoring Thresholds

**Transaction Value Thresholds:**
- SGD 5,000: Record keeping required
- SGD 20,000: Enhanced monitoring
- SGD 100,000: Senior review required
- SGD 500,000: Board notification

**Velocity Thresholds:**
- Daily transaction volume exceeding 5x average
- Weekly volume exceeding 3x average
- Rapid succession of transactions (>10 in 24 hours)

## 7. Suspicious Transaction Reporting

### 7.1 Suspicious Activity Indicators

**Transaction-Based Indicators:**
- Structuring to avoid reporting thresholds
- Transactions with no apparent economic purpose
- Unusual patterns or frequency
- Use of multiple wallets or addresses
- Rapid movement of funds
- Transactions inconsistent with stated purpose

**Customer-Based Indicators:**
- Reluctance to provide information
- Provision of false or misleading information
- Unusual concern with compliance procedures
- Unwillingness to proceed when asked for information
- Connections to high-risk jurisdictions

**NFT-Specific Indicators:**
- Transactions significantly above or below market value
- Rapid buying and selling of same NFTs
- Use of NFTs with no apparent artistic or collectible value
- Self-trading or wash trading patterns
- Layered transactions through multiple intermediaries

### 7.2 STR Filing Process

**Decision Process:**
1. Analyst identifies suspicious activity
2. Investigation and evidence gathering
3. Compliance Officer review
4. Determination of suspicion threshold
5. STR filing decision

**Filing Requirements:**
- File with STRO within prescribed timeframe
- Include all relevant information
- Maintain confidentiality (no tipping off)
- Document decision-making process
- Retain all supporting documentation

**Post-Filing:**
- Continue to monitor customer activity
- Update STR if new information emerges
- Cooperate with law enforcement investigations
- Maintain transaction relationship unless instructed otherwise

### 7.3 Confidentiality and Tipping Off

**Prohibition on Disclosure:**
- Do not disclose STR filing to customer
- Do not disclose to other parties except as legally required
- Limit internal disclosure to need-to-know basis
- Maintain confidentiality of investigations

**Legal Protections:**
- Good faith STR filing provides legal protection
- No liability for disclosure in STR
- Protection from civil or criminal liability

## 8. Sanctions Compliance

### 8.1 Sanctions Lists

**Applicable Lists:**
- United Nations Security Council Consolidated List
- Singapore's Terrorism (Suppression of Financing) Act
- OFAC Specially Designated Nationals (SDN) List
- EU Consolidated Financial Sanctions List
- Other relevant jurisdictional lists

### 8.2 Screening Process

**Initial Screening:**
- Name screening at onboarding
- Address screening (blockchain and physical)
- Entity screening for corporate customers
- Beneficial owner screening

**Ongoing Screening:**
- Daily batch screening of customer database
- Real-time transaction screening
- Immediate screening upon list updates
- Screening of counterparties

### 8.3 Handling Matches

**True Match Procedures:**
1. Freeze transaction immediately
2. Notify Compliance Officer
3. Conduct enhanced investigation
4. Report to MAS and relevant authorities
5. Document all actions
6. Block account pending resolution

**False Positive Resolution:**
- Document basis for false positive determination
- Obtain secondary review
- Update screening parameters if needed
- Clear transaction to proceed
